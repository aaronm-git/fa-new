  • Where is Flying Academy Europe located?

    Flying Academy is located in the Czech Republic with bases in Prague - the capital, Brno – second biggest city in the country and Ostrava.

    The Czech Republic is a member of EASA and the European Union.

    If you come from other EU states, you do not need a visa. Coming from non-EU states you will need to request a Schengen visa. Our staff will be able to assist you in this and provide an official Invitation letter.

  • What do I need to be able to start the training?

    To start your training you will need the right visa [if a visa is needed], health or travel insurance, proof of identity and diploma or certificate of your highest education. We will also schedule you for the Medical examination as soon as possible so that you’ll be able to fly solo. Find out more about the medical.

  • When can I start my training?

    You can start your training as soon as you have all the needed documents.

  • Are your planes safe?

    The regulation in the European Union is very strict about aviation safety. Our fleet is maintained at the highest levels of safety.

  • What is the difference between Part 61 and Part 141?

    In the United States, there are two paths as to which one can pursue their piloting endeavors

    • Pt.141
    • Pt.61

    Depending on what the student wants from flight training, he/she will be placed accordingly.

    Part 141

    • This form of training is structured with an outline on how to present the training to the student. A syllabus (created by the FAA) is provided for every course in which an instructor will read and interpret accordingly.
    • Those who pursue a professional pilot career tend to go for this sort of training.

    Part 61

    • This form of training is rather flexible compared to Pt 141, yet generally requires more hours of training.
    • This path is generally chosen for those who just want to acquire one certification. This does not exclude those who want to become professional pilots.
